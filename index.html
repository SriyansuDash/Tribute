Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam - The People's President
-Known as MISSILE MAN OF INDIA

ABOUT HIM:

--Being Born in 15 October 1931
--Born and raised in a Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
--Kalam was elected as the president of India in 2002 with the support of both the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party.
-- He was widely referred to as the "People's President".
--While delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, Kalam collapsed and died from an apparent cardiac arrest on 27 July 2015, aged 83.

MAJOR ACHIVEMENTS

Scientific Contributions
Played pivotal roles in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests and the development of Agni and Prithvi missiles.

Presidency(2002-2007)
Served as the 11th President of India, earning the title "People's President" for his accessibility and connection with citizens.

Awards & Honors
Received Bharat Ratna (1997), Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and numerous international honors.

Author & Teacher
Wrote several inspirational books including "Wings of Fire" and dedicated his post-presidency life to teaching students.
